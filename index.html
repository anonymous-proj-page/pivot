Abstract

Post-training with real-world experience is critical for reliable deployment of robot foundation models, but heterogeneous data from demonstrations, human corrections, and autonomous rollouts are difficult to convert into stable policy updates. We present PIVOT, an iterative real-world post-training system built on Trust-Region Flow Matching Optimization (TRFO). TRFO is a unified policy-improvement objective that assigns signed advantages to individual transitions and modulates the flow-matching loss accordingly: high-value behaviors are attracted, whereas low-value behaviors are repelled within a trust region. At each iteration, we first collect on-robot data using the current policy, estimate transition-level values via distributional TD learning, and then apply TRFO to update the policy. The updated policy is subsequently redeployed on the robot to gather the next round of experience, forming an iterative post-training procedure. On a suite of precise, contact-rich bimanual manipulation tasks, PIVOT yields consistent, monotonic gains over successive iterations, substantially improving success rates and throughput across all evaluated tasks.

Keywords: Real-World RL · Bimanual Manipulation · Post-Training

Continuous Task Demonstrations

After post-training, a single policy runs unattended over long horizons across four contact-rich bimanual tasks, each requiring fine-grained contact control and recovery from small execution errors.

Generalization

We probe visual generalization on objects with appearances never seen during training: the Laundry policy folds unseen T-shirts, and the Zip-Tie policy threads colored cable ties. Both indicate that the learned contact-rich skills transfer beyond the training distribution.

Robustness

We stress-test the post-trained policies under two perturbation regimes: physical disturbances applied mid-execution, and varied lighting conditions. The policies recover from disturbances and remain reliable under lighting shifts, on both Laundry and Zip-Tie.
